How a gap becomes a bet
No predictions. No picks. Just the inefficiency between two venues that price the same event differently.
01 — Normalize

One probability scale

Polymarket quotes cents, Kalshi quotes percent, sportsbooks quote American odds. Everything gets converted to a single 0–100% implied probability so prices are directly comparable.

02 — Compare

Measure the gap

For each shared event, SKEW lines up both venues. When their implied probabilities diverge, that spread is a real, capturable edge — buy YES where it's cheap, NO where it's dear.

SKEW is an analytics and research tool. It does not place bets, hold funds, or give financial advice. Prices shown here are illustrative sample data, not a live feed (see the wiring note below to connect real APIs). Cross-venue edges are real but modest and not risk-free in practice — the main risk is execution: getting one leg filled but not the other. Prediction-market availability and legality vary by state and platform; Kalshi has faced legal challenges in several states over whether its sports markets count as sports betting.
